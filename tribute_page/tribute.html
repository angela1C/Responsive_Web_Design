Hanna Sheehy-Skeffington

An Irish Suffragette.

Hanna_Sheehy-Skeffington - a suffragette in early 20th century Ireland.

  • 1877 - Born Hanna Sheehy in Kanturk, County Cork, Ireland
  • 1903 - Received a Master of Arts, following a Bachelor of Arts in 1899.
  • 1903 - Married Francis Skeffington, writer and activist. The couple took the surname Sheehy-Skeffington as a symbol of their honour to one another.
  • 1908 - A founding member of the Irish Women's Franchise League whose aim was to obtain women's voting rights.
  • 1911 - A founding member of the Irish Women Workers' Union - a trade union for women workers.
  • 1912 - Arrested, along with 7 other women, for smashing the glass windows of Dublin Castle.
  • 1912- Launched the Irish Citizen newspaper along with Margaret Cousins. The motto of the paper was "For Men and Women Equally The Rights of Citizenship; For Men and Women Equally The Duties of Citizenship"
  • 1913 - Worked with other suffragettes during the 1913 lock-put providing food for the families of the strikers.
  • 1916 - Husband Francis was shot dead during the 1916 Easter Rising having being arrested by British Soldiers.
  • 1926 - Attended the Women's International League for Peace and Freedom annual conference held in Dublin in July 1926
  • 1933 - Entered Northern Ireland to speak on behalf of female republican prisoners held in Armagh jail
  • 1935 - Spoke out against the Conditions of Employment Bill on behalf of the the Women's Graduates' Associations which was set to restrict employment opportunities for women.
  • 1937 - Founded the Women's Social and Progressive League to inform women of anti-women legislation which was being attempted to pass through the Dail.
  • 19?46 - died, aged 68 in Dublin, Ireland and was buried with her husband Francis in Glasnevin Cemetery.

"Until the women of Ireland are free, the men will not achieve emancipation."
